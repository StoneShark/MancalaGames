Mancala Game Configurations

A sampling of Mancala games that can be configured with the Manacala Game Engine are predefined. Descriptions are provided in the sections below.

If you are new to Mancala, Wari and Oware are good places to start. Kalah is a simple cross capture game.

Erherhe, Cow and Qelat add interesting complications to the basic rules. Erherhe introduces the concept of playing in rounds. Cow introduces varying the sow direction. Qelat adds the idea of hole ownership, in this case, called walda's.

Good introductions to multilap sowing are provided by Je ki n je, Endodoi and Ayoayo.

Not all games have the goal of capturing the most seeds. Deka and others have a goal of depriving your opponent of seeds. Weg and others involve capturing territory.

Several games isolate unique game parameters:

  • NoCapt: Captures are only accomplished by sowing into your own store.
  • NoSides: Players are not limited to starting a move on their own side of the board, instead a player may start a move from any hole.
  • NoSidesChild: Players do not own the holes on their own side of the board; instead ownership is claimed (i.e. making a child) by sowing a hole to four seeds.
  • SameSide: players only sow on their own side of the board. Captured seeds are placed in an opponent's hole.
  • SowOpDirs: Players sow in opposite directions. The first move of each game defines the directions for each player.

Personal Note: These configurations are my best understanding of the rules from various sources. I cannot be certain that I have faithfully reproduced any game. You may know a particular set of rules by a different name. You may have a particular "house rule" that varies from the specific configuration provided. I might simply have an implementation error. Please consider these files a starting point for defining your own favorite games.

The Game / Parameter Cross Reference table can be used to determine if a particular set of rules has a different name than you expect.

Adi

A multi-lap mancala played in rounds. The goal is to claim ownership of at least 10 holes. Each player initially owns the holes on their side of the board. Owners of holes are updated at the start of each round based on the number of seeds captured in the previous round.

Captures are on 4s on the final sown seed by the sower (regardless of hole owner). The seeds of any hole sown to 4 are captured by the hole's owner (regardless of sower).

A player must pass if they have no moves. If neither player has moves, the game is over.

  • see Lam Waladach

Adji-boto

Sow single-lap, CCW. Leave one seed in the move start hole. The minimum move is 2 seeds. Captures may not be made on the first move by either player.

Each of the first 10 moves must start from a different hole. This is accomplished by using the unlock mechanism, moving from a hole unlocks it. Until all of a player's holes are unlocked, moves may only start from locked holes.

If a player does not have a move, they must pass.

Captures are made when the hole after the final seed sown contains 1, 3 or 5 seeds and continue as long as holes contain 1, 3, or 5 seeds. Those seeds (in the next hole) are captured, but not any from the final hole sown. These captures do not check the lock condition.

The game ends when one player has accumulated more than half of the seeds or when neither player can move. Unclaimed seeds go to the hole owners.

Bechi

A single-lap mancala game. Moves may only start from holes with two or more seeds. The start hole determines sow direction of sow (left holes: CW, right holes: CCW). The first seed is sown in the start hole (that is, it is left in the hole).

Captures are on evens on either side of the board. Multiple captures proceed in the sow direction. Captures may not occur from locked holes and a locked hole ends a multiple capture. Holes are unlocked when a move is started from the hole.

If a player has no valid move, they must pass.

Games are played in rounds. A round ends when neither player has a valid move.

To setup the next round, seeds from each player's store are distributed 4 per hole for as many holes as can be completely filled on the player's side of the board. The player's remaining seeds are put into each player's store. Unfilled holes are out of play for the round, but may be put back into play in subsequent rounds. By default, the holes are filled inward from each side; if desired, the blocked holes can be changed by the player. The player that moved last in a round, starts the next round.

A game is over when either player cannot fill a single hole, i.e. has fewer than four seeds.

Cow

  • Moves started from the left 2 holes will sow CW. Either mouse button maybe used.
  • Moves started from the right 2 holes will sow CCW. Either mouse button maybe used.

Dakon

A multi-lap mancala played in rounds. Sow direction is counter-clockwise, sow into one's own store. A player may move again when the final seed is sown into their store.

If a player has no seeds at the start of their turn, they must pass.

Cross capture from opponent's side only; pick own seed on capture.

A round ends when one player has sufficient seeds for a game win (71 or more) or all seeds have been moved into the stores.

To setup the next round, seeds from each player's store are distributed 7 per hole for as many holes as can be completely filled on the player's side of the board. The player's remaining seeds are put into the player's store. Unfilled holes are out of play for the round, but may be put back into play in subsequent rounds. The player that moved last in a round, starts the next round.

The game is over when either player cannot fill 4 holes.

Variations:

Capture on evens that are greater than or equal to 4.

If the final seed of a sow brings a hole to three seeds, a tuzdek is made (child). A tuzdek cannot be made on rightmost hole on the opponent's side--this would be the last hole before sowing would continue back to your own side of the board. Tuzdeks cannot be directly opposite eachother.

Variation:

  • Toguz Kumalak is very similar.

Urim

A single-lap mancala, sow direction is counter-clockwise, capture on opponents side on 2s, 3s and 4s.

A sow which cycles the board, will skip the hole that the move started from. Holes may never be sown above 17 seeds; skip such holes while sowing. If your opponent has no seeds, you must make seeds available to them (if you can).

If a grand slam were to occur, the seeds are left on the board, that is, they are not captured.

A round ends when the current player cannot move or when the outcome cannot change. A cycling round can be ended by the End Round command on the Game menu, any seeds left in play go to the owners of the hole they are in.

The game ends when one player scores 10 points: 1 point for each win and another point for a skunk, i.e. collecting 36 or more seeds for the win. The Game Tally, available to be displayed via the Display menu, shows progress toward that goal.

Deviation: Per the rules: after the first capture on 2 or 3 subsequent captures (multicapt) are made on 2, 3 or 4 seed holes. Mancala Games only supports the capture mechanism being the same for the first and multiple captures.

Wari

A single-lap mancala, sow direction is counter-clockwise. A sow which cycles the board, will skip the hole that the move started from. If your opponent has no seeds, you must make seeds available to them (if you can).

Captures are on 2s and 3s from the opponent's side (final seed sown makes either 2 or 3). As long as there is a an unbroken group of holes containing 2 or 3 seeds immediately preceding the final hole sown, these holes are also captured.

  • captures on various numbers of seeds
  • a single seed may not be used for capture, NOSINGLECAPT

Weg

A territory game that uses both stores and wegs (children); capturing 10 holes wins. Sow is counter-clockwise and multi-lap. Wegs are made when a final seed sows a hole to 4 seeds in your opponent's territory.

Captures are on the final seed being sown into an opponent's weg. The sown seed and one more (if there is one) are captured to your store. Repeat turn on capture (but not after making a weg).

Played in rounds with start player alternating.

Variations:

  • 2x6 with 3 start seeds
  • 2x10 with 4 start seeds
  • not played in rounds
  • no repeat turn on capture

ZigZag

A semi-multi-lap mancala.

Sow in a zigzag, back-and-forth across the board, towards the center-line of the board. At either end, sow directly across the board. Continue in multiple laps as long as the last seed of a lap lands on your own side of the board. See help for details and examples.

A move of a single seed across the center-line of the board that does not capture may not be moved back on the very next move.

Cross capture on own side of the board--final seed ends in empty hole on opponents side of the board--collect your own seeds. Leave the seed effecting the capture.

Goal: capture the most seeds.

Invented by Clark D. Rodeffer and Amanda J.L. Rodeffer.

Variations:

  • any even number of holes
  • any odd number of seeds

Start seeds: 5
