Mancala Game Configurations

A sampling of Mancala games that can be configured with the Manacala Game Engine are predefined. Descriptions are provided in the sections below.

If you are new to Mancala, Wari and Oware are good places to start. Kalah is a simple cross capture game.

Erherhe, Cow and Qelat add interesting complications to the basic rules. Erherhe introduces the concept of playing in rounds. Cow introduces varying the sow direction. Qelat adds the idea of hole ownership, in this case, called walda's.

Good introductions to multilap sowing are provided by Endodoi and Ayoayo.

Not all games have the goal of capturing the most seeds. Deka and others have a goal of depriving your opponent of seeds. Weg and others involve capturing territory.

Several games isolate unique game parameters:

  • NoCapt: Captures are only accomplished by sowing into your own store.
  • NoSides: Players are not limited to starting a move on their own side of the board, instead a player may start a move from any hole.
  • NoSidesChild: Players do not own the holes on their own side of the board; instead ownership is claimed (i.e. making a child) by sowing a hole to four seeds.
  • SowOpDirs: Players sow in opposite directions. The first move of each game defines the directions for each player.

Personal Note: These configurations are my best understanding of the rules from various sources. I cannot be certain that I have faithfully reproduced any game. You may know a particular set of rules by a different name. You may have a particular "house rule" that varies from the specific configuration provided. I might simply have an implementation error. Please consider these files a starting point for defining your own favorite games.

The Game / Parameter Cross Reference table can be used to determine if a particular set of rules has a different name than you expect.

Ayoayo

A multi-lap mancala; sow direction counter-clockwise skipping start hole on full cycle sow; cross-capture from opponents side when a sow ends in own empty hole. If your opponent has no seeds, you must make seeds available to them (if you can).

The game ends when one player captures more than half the seeds; when the current player cannot share seeds to an opponent without seeds; or when there is only one seed left on the board.

Holes per side: 6

Start seeds: 4

Goal: MAX_SEEDS

Cross-Capture: Yes

Multilap Sow: LAPPER

Must Share: Yes

Opp Side Capt Only: Yes

Skip Start on Lap: Yes

Sow Direction: CCW

Stores: Yes

Own Seed on Xcapture: PICK_ON_CAPT

Rules: Mohr, Merilyn Simonds. The New Games Treasury. 1993. p102.

Bao (Tanzanian)

The starting player may choose to rearrange the seeds on their side of the board. The opponent's side will be arranged in the same manner from their perspective. If the player choses to rearrange the board, it counts as their opening move. If they do not so choose, they cannot make a child or capture until their opponent does either.

Sow is multi-lap in a counter-clockwise direction. Sowing the final seed of a lap to 4 on opposite side, creates a child which will collect seedd but can't be captured. A child may not be created with a single seed in a move that was initiated from the player's rightmost hole. Moves may not start from children.

Cross-capture on opponents side. On capture, take the seed initiating the capture. Multiple captures may be made in sow direction as long as the player's side holes are empty. Seeds from child holes may not be captured.

Played in rounds. Each round ends when neither player has a move. At the start of each round, the board is resized to the number of holes that the player with fewer seeds can fill completely.

If the board size is reduced to 3 or less per side, no children maybe created.

Bechi

A single-lap mancala game. Moves may only be started from holes with two or more seeds. The start hole determines sow direction of sow (left holes: CW, right holes: CCW). The first seed is sown in the start hole (that is, it is left in the hole).

Captures are on evens on either side of the board. Multiple captures proceed in the sow direction. Captures may not occur from locked holes. Holes are unlocked when a move is started from the hole.

If a player has no valid move, they must pass.

Games are played in rounds. A round ends when neither player has a valid move.

To setup the next round, seeds from each player's store are distributed 4 per hole for as many holes as can be completely filled on the player's side of the board. The player's remaining seeds are put into each player's store. Unfilled holes are out of play for the round, but may be put back into play in subsequent rounds. By default the holes are filled inward from each side; if desired, the blocked holes can be changed by the player. The player that moved last in a round, starts the next round.

A game is over when either player cannot fill a single hole, i.e. has fewer than four seeds.

Start seeds: 6

Move Unlock for Capture: Yes

Multiple Captures: Yes

Must Pass: Yes

Round Fill Method: UCHOOSE

Round Starter: ALTERNATE

Rounds: NO_MOVES

Sow Direction: SPLIT

Sow Start Hole: Yes

Stores: Yes

Rules: Russ, Laurence. Mancala Games. 1984. p22.

Cow

A single-lap mancala with a SPLIT SOW_DIRECT. The sow direction depends on start location:

  • Moves started from the left 2 holes will sow CW. Either mouse button maybe used.
  • Moves started from the right 2 holes will sow CCW. Either mouse button maybe used.
  • For the center hole, the mouse button used to start the move selects the sow direction. The left mouse button will sow CW and the right mouse button will sow CCW.

Two seeds are required for a move. A player must pass, if they have no moves.

Captures are on 2s and 4s on the opponents side with multiple captures occuring in the preceeding holes. Specifically, the capture direction is the reverse of the sow direction used for the move.

The games ends when one player has more than half the seeds or when neither player can move.

Holes per side: 5

Start seeds: 5

Goal: MAX_SEEDS

Capture On Values: 2 4

Minimum Seeds for Move: 2

Multiple Captures: Yes

Must Pass: Yes

Opp Side Capt Only: Yes

Sow Direction: SPLIT

Stores: Yes

User Chooses Direction: 2

Rules: Russ, Laurence. Mancala Games. 1984. p21.

Dakon

A multi-lap mancala played in rounds. Sow direction is counter-clockwise, sow into ones own store. A player may move again when the final seed is sown into their store. Cross capture from opponents side only; pick own seed on capture. If a player has no seeds at the start of their turn, they must pass.

A round ends when all seeds have been moved into the stores. To setup the next round, seeds from each player's store are distributed 7 per hole for as many holes as can be completely filled on the player's side of the board. The player's remaining seeds are put into the player's store. Unfilled holes are out of play for the round, but may be put back into play in subsequent rounds. The player that moved last in a round, starts the next round.

The game is over when either player cannot fill a 4 holes.

Variations:

  • played with 6 to 10 holes per side with the number of start seeds equaling number of holes.
  • passing disallowed - a player with no moves at the start of their turn loses.

Deka

The goal is to deprive the opponent of seeds. Sow counter-clockwise with multiple laps (LAPPER). After the first turn, holes are closed on opponent side when the final seed of a lap brings the seeds in a hole to 3. Sowing then continues with those 3 seeds. Each player's rightmost hole may not be closed. In subsequent sowing of closed holes (blocked), own holes are skipped but seeds for opponent's holes are taken out of play.

The Deka board is not a 2 rank board, but hole assignment yields itself to moving the middle end holes to the respective player's side thus making it a 2 rank board.

Variations:

  • 6 to 8 holes per side
  • 2 to 4 start seeds
  • allow closing of rightmost holes (SOW_RULE of SOW_BLKD_DIV)

Holes per side: 6

Start seeds: 2

Goal: DEPRIVE

Blocks: Yes

General Input: 3

Multilap Sow: LAPPER

No Capture First Move: Yes

Sow Direction: CCW

Special Sow Rules: SOW_BLKD_DIV_NR

Rules: Russ, Laurence. Mancala Games. 1984. p40.

Erherhe

A single-lap mancala, sow direction is counter-clockwise, multiple capture on opponents side on 1s and 3s. A sow which cycles the board, will skip the hole that the move started from. If your opponent has no seeds, you must make seeds available to them (if you can).

Erherhe is played in rounds. A round ends when either player has more than half of the seeds. Players then collect the seeds remaining on their side of the board. To setup the next round, each player fills as many holes as they can with 4 seeds. Left over seeds are placed in their store (3 or fewer for loser). The looser's holes that cannot be filled are out of play for the round. The loser starts new round.

The game is over when either player cannot fill two holes; that is, the winner will have collected all but 1 to 3 seeds.

Variation:

  • Another value for ROUND_STARTER
  • Another value for ROUND_FILL

Giuthi

A multilap mancala in which the current player chooses the sow direction for the first lap; subsequent laps alternate sow direction. Moves may only start from holes with 2 or more seeds; if a player cannot move they must pass.

Cross-capture from opponents side, but only if seeds have been sown on the opposite side of the board on the current turn. On capture, pick own seed. Multiple captures may be done in same direction as the final lap sowed.

If a player ends a turn on their side of the board in an empty hole but without having sown the opposite side, they get another move.

The round ends if neither player can move. The loser of each round, can rearrange the seeds on their side of the board with the restriction that each hole must contain at least one seed and that they have a valid move. Before the round is started the same pattern will be reflected on the winner's side.

The game ends when either player does not have enough seeds to fill their side of the board with a valid move (i.e. one hole with MIN_MOVE seeds and others with at least one seed).

Variations:

  • Played as a single game (no rounds).
  • Board sizes 6 to 10 holes per side.
  • Start seeds varies from 4 to 9 (smaller board, fewer start seeds).
  • Some combinations of size and start seeds are quite unbanalnced.
  • Instead of minimum move of 2, singletons may start moves if all holes are empty or contain one seed (allow_rule == SINGLE_ONLY_ALL).

Holes per side: 8

Start seeds: 6

Goal: MAX_SEEDS

Capture in Sow Direction: Yes

Cross-Capture: Yes

Minimum Seeds for Move: 2

Multilap Sow: LAPPER

Multiple Captures: Yes

Must Pass: Yes

Opp Side Capt Only: Yes

Round Fill Method: UMOVE

Round Starter: WINNER

Rounds: NO_MOVES

Sow Direction: CCW

Special Sow Rules: CHANGE_DIR_LAP

Stores: Yes

User Chooses Direction: 0 1 2 3 4 5 6 7

Must sow opp for xcapt: Yes

Own Seed on Xcapture: PICK_ON_CAPT

Rules: Russ, Laurence. Mancala Games. 1984. p37.

Deviation: Game doesn't end until <= 4 seeds for loser, but in rearrangement rules, all holes must contain a seed. Also, the winner's side is setup reversed of loser's side, so both players must have enough seeds for a valid move. Therefore, the game will end when there are not enough seeds for the loser to fill their side of the board with a valid move.

Kalah

A single lap mancala with a counter-clockwise sow direction. When sowing past the right edge of the board, sow a seed into your own store (SOW_OWN_STORE). Do not sow into your opponent's store.

If the last seed ends in an empty hole on the player's own side of the board, capture any seeds from the opposite hole (CROSSCAPT). When performing a capture, take the final single seed sown as well. Pick your own seed only when opponents seeds are captured.

If a player's sow ends in their own store, they take another turn (repeat turn).

If a player has no seeds at the start of their turn, the game is over: the opponent collects the remainder of their seeds and the winner is determined based on final seed count.

Variation:

  • Require must share (MUSTSHARE) because it removes the seed hoarding strategy.

Lagerung

Played in rounds. A round ends when neither player can move. The loser of previous round starts the next round. The game is over when either player cannot fill at least 4 holes (GPARAM_ONE).

Lam Waladach

A multi-lap mancala played in rounds. The goal is to claim ownership of at least 9 holes. Each player initially owns the holes on their side of the board. Owners of holes are updated at the start of each round based on the number of seeds captured in the previous round.

The seeds of any hole sown to 4 are captured by the hole's owner (regardless of sower). Captures are on 4s on the final sown seed by the sower (regardless of hole owner). A capture results in a repeat turn. Captures and pick 4s are allowed on the first move of the game. A player must pass, if they have no moves.

If neither player has moves, the game is over. The game also ends if there are fewer that 6 seeds in play, with the seeds going to the round starter.

Special Sow Rules: OWN_SOW_CAPT_ALL

Sow Direction: CCW

Stores: Yes

Rules: Russ, Laurence. Mancala Games. 1984. p55. (with liberties)

Deviation: Both captures and pick 4s are allowed on the first turn. The Mancala Game engine doesn't allow doing one of these, but not the other. The game ends when fewer than 6 seeds are in play, with the round starter taking these last seeds.

Note: Hole ownership of > 9, results very many rounds and long games.

Longbeu-a-cha

Sow counter-clockwise via the multi-lap next-hole method (MLAPS of LAPPER_NEXT).

While either player is sowing, both players capture all holes sown to 4 on their side of the board.

When the hole from which a new lap should start is empty, capture any seeds in the hole following the empty hole. That is, captures are made when the hole after the hole in which the final seed of a lap was sown, is empty and there are seeds in the hole 'two out' from the last sown seed.

Captures continue while holes are alternately empty and occupied in the counter-clockwise direction.

Unlike Pallam Kuzhi, this game is not played in rounds.

Variations:

  • SOW_RULE of SOW_SOW_CAPT_ALL instead of OWN_SOW_CAPT_ALL
  • See Olinda, Pallam Kuzhi, and Pandi.

NamNam

A multi-lap mancala played in rounds. The goal is to claim ownership of at least 10 holes. Each player initially owns the holes on their side of the board. Owners of holes are updated at the start of each round based on the number of seeds captured in the previous round.

Captures are on 4s on the final sown seed by the sower (regardless of hole owner). The seeds of any hole sown to 4 are captured by the hole's owner (regardless of sower). On capture, if there are 4 or fewer seeds left on the board the capture takes them as well.

If, at the start of your turn, your opponent has no seeds; you must make seeds available to them, if you can, otherwise the game is over.

Sow Direction: CCW

Stores: Yes

Olinda

The starting player chooses the sow direction that they will use for the game; the second player will use the opposite direction for the game. Sowing is done in multiple laps from the next hole (MLAPS of LAPPER_NEXT).

While either player is sowing, both players capture all holes sown to 4 on their side of the board. Specifically, the non-sower may capture seeds from holes sown to 4 on their own side of the board.

When the hole from which a new lap should start is empty, capture any seeds in the hole following the empty hole. That is, captures are made when the hole after the hole in which the final seed of a lap was sown, is empty and there are seeds in the hole 'two out' from the last sown seed.

Captures continue while holes are alternately empty and occupied in the player's sow direction.

Played in ROUNDS. Each round ends when the current player does not have any playable holes. Round setup is via the Empty Hole Method of ROUND_FILL using the OUTSIDE_FILL approach which the user may choose to override (UCHOOSE). After the next round setup unfilled holes are out of play. The players alternate starting rounds.

The game is over when either player cannot fill a single hole.

Variations:

  • SOW_RULE of SOW_SOW_CAPT_ALL instead of OWN_SOW_CAPT_ALL
  • Other options for ROUND_STARTER and ROUND_FILL.
  • See Longbeu-a-cha, Pallam Kuzhi, and Pandi.

Holes per side: 7

Start seeds: 4

Goal: MAX_SEEDS

Capture in Sow Direction: Yes

Capture On Values: 4

Capture Two Out: Yes

Multilap Sow: LAPPER_NEXT

Multiple Captures: Yes

Rounds: NO_MOVES

Round Fill Method: UCHOOSE

Round Starter: ALTERNATE

Sow Direction: PLAYALTDIR

Special Sow Rules: OWN_SOW_CAPT_ALL

Stores: Yes

Rules: Russ, Laurence. Mancala Games. 1984. p61.
